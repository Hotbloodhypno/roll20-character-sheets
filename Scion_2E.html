PATHS

• Define three Paths for your character. The first Path corresponds to your character’s origin, who they were before the story started. The second corresponds to their role, who they are now (your character’s concept is usually a good fit here). The third corresponds to their pantheon, how they relate to the gods and myths of their patron’s pantheon.
• A short description of the Path (e.g. “Moscow Ballet Veteran” or “Field Primatologist”)
• Three Skills associated with the Path. Which three Skills you choose are up to you, but you should be prepared to justify non-obvious choices like Culture for “Raised by Wolves.” Two of the Skills you choose for your character’s Pantheon Path must be the Pantheon Skills for divine patron’s pantheon. Any given Skill can be associated with at most two of your Paths.
• A Path Condition that triggers when you invoke the Path too often. You can choose one of the example Conditions or use them as a template to create your own.

•Connections... Contact, Group, Access.
• The group connection is a well-defined collection of people who share a similar Path or are directly related.
• The access connection is the equipment or specialized space related to the Path
• The contact connection is a single person related to the Path and with whom the character has a relationship.
• A character gains two points per invocation to use to create her Contacts. She can spend these dots on either gaining additional Contacts, or making a single Contact better.
• Each Contact begins with one dot and one tag (see list of suggested tags below). Each additional point placed in a single Contact adds an additional tag.
• You can call upon your Comnnections up to 4 times a session.. but if you call upon them more than twice per session, it will cause Complicatiions.

SKILLS

• Prioritize your characters’ Paths as primary, secondary, and tertiary.
• Your character receives three dots in each Skill associated with their primary Path, two dots in each Skill associated with their secondary path, and one dot in each Skill associated with their tertiary path. These dots are cumulative for Skills associated with two Paths.
• Place 10 dots in any Skills.
• For every Skill that ends up with three or more dots, choose a Specialty.

ATTRIBUTES

• Your character begins with a single dot in all Attributes for free.
• Choose one of the three Approaches (Force, Finesse, Fortitude). Add two dots to each Attribute in that Approach.

• Next, prioritize the three Arenas (Physical, Mental, Social) as primary, secondary, or tertiary. Assign six dots to Attributes in the primary category, four to the secondary, and two to the tertiary. Divide these dots however you like, but no Attribute can be higher than five.

CALLLING / KNACKS

Callings :
• Choose three Callings (Creator, Guardian, Healer, Hunter, Judge, Leader, Liminal, Lover, Sage, Trickster, Warrior).
• At least one of your character’s Callings must come from their divine patron’s three Favored Callings.

• Your character receives two dots in two of their Callings and one dot in the other one.
• For each Calling, choose a number of Heroic Knacks equal to the Calling’s rating. Alternately, you may choose a single Immortal Knack instead of two Heroic Knacks for your character’s two-dot Calling.
• For each dot you have to chose a Keyword (p 217+ Hero)
• If your character is a creature of Legend, you may also choose Knacks appropriate to their nature.
• Fatebinding Role - three for each of your three chosen Callings

• Either add 2 other Knacks >or add 5 more points in Birthrights.>

BIRTHRIGHTS

• Your character receives seven dots ( and 5 more !) to allocate to Birthrights. You may choose from the example Birthrights in this book, or design your own.
• Most Scions receive Birthrights tied to their patron’s pantheon, but the Overworld is a cosmopolitan place — not only do the pantheons routinely talk and trade amongst themselves, many Gods are members of multiple pantheons.
• If you have an interesting idea for a story about, say, a Scion of Sun Wukong who carries the Spear of Lugh, don’t let pantheon affiliation stand in your way.

BOONS

• Choose two Boons from any of your character’s Purviews, whether innate or granted by Birthrights.

WEAPONS

• A weapon may have up to three total points of tags. Knack 'Master of Weapons' increases the Tags by the points in Warrior Calling.
• Tags: (e.g.) aggrivated 2, arcing 1, automatic 2, bashing 0, concealing 1, firearm 0, grappling 1, leathal 0, long range 1, loud -1, melee 0, messy -1, piercing 2, pushing 1, ranged 0, reach 1, reaturning 1, shockwave 4, slow -1, stun 1, throw 0, two-handed -1, unconsealable -1, versitile 2, worn 2.

ARMOUR

• Armour can have up to three points in total in tags.
• Tags: (e.g.) cumbersome -1, consealable 2, hard 1-3, innocuous 2, restraint 2, soft 1, weighty -1.

MOMENTUM

• Momentum is stored in a single pool shared by all players (called the Black Pool), which can hold up to (twice the number of players) at a time.
• What Grants Momentum
• Consolation from a failed roll: 1
• Failed Specialty: +1
• Botch: +2
• Certain Knacks, divine relics, or Purviews can additionally grant Momentum to the pool.
